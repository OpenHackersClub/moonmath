Topology is the study of properties preserved under continuous deformation — the mathematics of nearness without measurement. These pages trace five pillars: compactness and its interplay with closedness and boundedness, convergence of sequences, fixed points of continuous maps, and the separation axioms that let us build continuous functions to order.
